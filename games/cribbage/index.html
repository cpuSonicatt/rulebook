How to play
Cribbage

You will need

  • A standard 52-card deck.
  • A cribbage board, or a pen-and-paper to keep score.

Terms

  • cribbage board - the board used to keep score.
  • the Box - the dealer's 2nd hand, which is revealed last. Cards in the box are usually placed near the dealer.

Deal and Discard

Choose a dealer by whichever means.


For 2 players

Deal 6 cards to each player.


For 3 players

Deal 5 cards to each player, and deal 1 card to the box.


For 4 players

Deal 5 cards to each player.


Each player should retain 4 cards, and discard the remainder to the box.

Set the rest of the cards to one side, face down.

Play

Note: face cards are valued at 10, and aces are valued at 1.

Play begins with the player on the dealer's left. Scoring opportunities are called-out in red. The game ends as soon as a player reaches 121.

Cut

The player to the left of the dealer cuts the remaining cards, and the dealer reveals the top card of the lower half and places it face up on the remaining card pile.

If the revealed card is a Jack, the dealer score 2 points.

Play

Starting with the player on the dealer's left, each player places a card and announces the count (the cumulative value of all the cards played so far). The count can't go over 31.

If a player plays a card to make the count equal 15, they score 2 points.

If a player plays a card to make the count equal 31, they score 1 point.

If a player plays the last card of a round, they score 1 point.

If a player plays a card to make a pair, a three-of-a-kind, or a four-of-a-kind, they score 2, 6, and 12 points, respectively.

If a player plays a card to make a run of 3 or more, they score the number of cards in the run.

A player must play a card if they're able to. If a player can't play a card, they pass.

Once no more cards can be played, the count resets and the already-played cards are turned over. This continues until all cards are played.

Show

Starting with the dealer's hand, going clockwise, and ending with the dealer's box, players reveal and score their cards:

Combinations of cards that add up to 15 scores 2 points each.

Combinations of 3 or more cards that form a consecutive run score the number of cards in the run.

A pair, three-of-a-kind, and four-of-a-kind score 2, 6 and 12 points respectively.

Four-card flushes (all 4 cards in your hand only, excluding the box) score 4 points. Five-card flushes (all 4 cards in your hand only plus the cut card) score 5 points.

Having a Jack of the same suit as the cut card scores 1 point.

If no player has won, the dealer changes to the next player.
