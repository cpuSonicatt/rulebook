How to play Cribbage

An English card game that utilises a peg board to keep score, and is the only card game legally playable in pubs without requiring permission from local authority. Due to the nature of scoring in Cribbage being instantaneous (instead of accumulated and applied at the end of each round), a cribbage board can come in handy.

You will need

  • A standard 52-deck of cards.
  • A cribbage board, or a pen-and-paper to keep score.

Terms

  • cribbage board - the board used to keep score.
  • the Box - the dealer's 2nd hand, which is revealed last. Cards in the box are usually placed near the dealer.

Deal and Discard

Choose a dealer by whichever means.

For 2 players

Deal 6 cards to each player, and both players discard 2 cards to the box.


For 3 players

Deal 5 cards to each player, deal 1 card to the box, and each player discard 1 card to the box.


For 4 players

Deal 5 cards to each player, and each player discard 1 card to the box.


Set the rest of the cards to one side, face down.

Each player's hand, and the box, should end up with 4 cards.

Play

Note: face cards are valued at 10, and aces are valued at 1.

Play begins with the player on the dealer's left. Scoring oppotunities are highlighted in red. The game ends as soon as a player reaches 121.

Cut

The player to the left of the dealer cuts the remaining cards, the dealer reveals the top card of the lower half, and places it face up on the remaining card pile.

If the revealed card is a Jack, the dealer gains 2 points.

Play

Starting with the player on the dealer's left, each player in turn places a card, and announces the count (the cumulative value of all the cards played so far). The count can't go over 31.

If a player plays a card to make the count equal 15, they gain 2 points.

If a player plays a card to make the count equal 31, they gain 1 point.

If a player plays the last card of a round, they gain 1 point.

If a player plays a card to make a pair, a three-of-a-kind, or four-of-a-kind, they gain 2 points, 6 points, and 12 points respectively.

If a player plays a card to make a run of 3 cards or more, they gain that number of cards in the run.

A player must play a card if they're able to. If a player can't play a card, they pass.

Once no more cards can be played, the count resets and the already-played cards are turned over. This continues until all cards are played.

Show

Starting with the dealer hand, going clockwise, and ending with the dealer's box, players reveal and score their cards:

Combinations of cards that add up to 15 score 2 points each.

Consecutve runs of 3 or more cards score 1 point per card in the run.

Pairs, three-of-a-kind, and four-of-a-kind scores 2, 6 and 12 points respectively.

Four-card flushes (all 4 cards in your hand only, excluding the box) scores 4 points. Five-card flushes (all 4 cards in your hand only plus the cut card) scores 5 points.

Having a Jack of the same suit as the cut card scores 1 point.

If no player has won, the dealer changes to the next player.
